What is Dim Sum ?


Pork and shrimp dim sum is a type of Chinese cuisine that consists of small, savory, bite-sized dumplings, typically served steamed. The filling is made from ground pork and shrimp, which are mixed with an array of flavorful seasonings, including soy sauce, rice wine, sesame oil, scallions, and cilantro. The filling is then wrapped in a thin layer of dough, which is often pleated to create a characteristic, purse-like shape. Once steamed, the dough becomes soft and pliant, while the filling retains its juicy texture and savory flavor. Ingrediants :-


  • 1/2 pound ground pork

  • 1/2 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined

  • 2 tablespoons chopped scallions

  • 2 tablespoons chopped cilantro

  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce

  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil

  • 1 tablespoon rice wine

  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch

  • 1 teaspoon sugar

  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

  • 1/4 teaspoon white pepper



STEP BY STEP METHOD :-


  1. In a medium bowl, mix together the ground pork, shrimp, scallions, cilantro, soy sauce, rice wine, sesame oil, cornstarch, sugar, salt, and white pepper until well combined.

  2. In a separate large bowl, mix together the flour, water, and salt until a dough forms.

  3. Roll the dough out on a lightly floured surface to 1/8 inch thickness.

  4. Using a round cookie cutter or biscuit cutter, cut the dough into circles.

  5. Place a small spoonful of the filling in the center of each dough circle, then pinch the edges together to form a pleated purse shape.

  6. Place the dumplings in a bamboo steamer basket and steam for 8-10 minutes until cooked through.

  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and serve hot.
