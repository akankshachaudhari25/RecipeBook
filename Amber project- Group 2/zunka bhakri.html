Making Zunka Bhakar - Easy and simple recipe


Jump to Recipe

Zunka Bhakar is a rustic food from the belly of Pune, a city that lies southeast of Bombay. A flatbread of millet flour is served alongside a stir-fry of vegetables with chickpea flour. It is spicy, savory, delicious, gluten-free and, of course, vegan.

What is Zunka Bhakri?


Zunka is a gram flour porridge or a gram flour curry, similar to polenta. It is a traditional Indian dish prepared in Maharashtra, North Karnataka, and Goa. It is also known by the name of pithla or pithle. The dish is also referred to as jhunka bhakar or pithla bhakri. Pithle is also known as chun in Vidarbha region of Maharashtra.

Bhakri is a popular Indian unleavened flatbread native to the Indian state of Maharashtra, although it is commonly found throughout Gujarat, Goa, and Rajasthan. It can be made with jowar, ragi, sorghum, wheat, or rice flour, all of them high in dietary fibers, making bhakri a healthy type of flatbread.

It is sometimes additionally flavored with ghee butter or cumin seeds, and served with chutneys, baingan bharta, vegetables, curry, or rice. Traditionally, bhakri was often consumed by the farmers for breakfast and lunch, providing sustenance throughout the long working days.

Since it tastes the best when the flour is freshly milled, some Indian cooks use a millstone before making the bread in order to enhance bhakri's texture and flavor.

Ingrediants for zunka :-


  • oil : 2 tbsp oil.
  • mustard : 1 tbsp mustard.
  • cumin / jeera : 1 tbsp cumin / jeera.
  • hing / asafoetida : pinch hing / asafoetida.
  • garlic : 4 clove (finely chopped).
  • chilli : 1 (finely chopped).
  • onion : 1 onion (finely chopped).
  • turmeric : ¼ tbsp turmeric.
  • kashmiri red chilli powder : 1 tsp kashmiri red chilli powder.
  • salt : use as per test.
  • besan / gram flour : 1 cup besan / gram flour.
  • water :water as required.
  • coriander : 2 tbsp coriander (finely chopped).

Ingrediants for Bhakri :-


  • Bajra, jowar, ragi, sorghum, wheat, or rice flour : 1 cup jowar, ragi, sorghum, wheat, or rice flour.
  • water : As per required.

How to make Zunka ?


  1. firstly, in a large kadai heat 2 tbsp oil and splutter 1 tsp mustard, 1 tsp cumin and pinch hing.

  2. also add 4 clove garlic, 1 chilli and saute well.

  3. now add 1 onion and saute until the change colour slightly.

  4. further add ¼ tsp turmeric, 1 tsp chilli powder and 1 tsp salt.

  5. saute slightly, without burning the spices.

  6. now add 1 cup besan and saute on low flame.

  7. saute for 5 minutes, or until the besan turns golden and aromatic.

  8. further, sprinkle ¼ cup water and mix well.

  9. keep sprinkling water in batches until the mixture turns moist.

  10. i have sprinkled almost 1 cup water in batches.

  11. cover and simmer for 10 minutes, or until the besan is cooked completely.

  12. give a good mix making sure there are no lumps.

  13. finally, add in coriander leaves and enjoy zunka with bhakri.


How to make Bhakri ?


  1. Take flour.

  2. Add water and mix well.

  3. Once the dough cools a bit, start to knead the dough and continue to knead for at least 5 minutes.

  4. For best results gather all dough and press it using the palms of your hand.

  5. Dust dry flour over the rolling board and start to pat it to flatten it into round bread or bhakri.

  6. Heat up skillet or tawa, place bhakri, the side on which we applied dry flour it should face upward.

  7. As soon as you place bhakri, apply water on the top of bhakri and spread water evenly all over bhakri.

  8. Roast it just till the time water starts to evaporate from the top side of bhakri.

  9. Now flip the bhakri and cook from other side for just 2 minutes. At this point in time, you can increase the gas heat.

  10. Now put the skillet away from gas heat, lift bhakri using tong and roast it on direct gas heat from both sides.
